Apply for New e-Khata<br>A property that does not currently hold an e-Khata must be registered for a new e-Khata upon the occurrence of any of the following:<br>Ownership Transfer: When property rights are transferred via a registered Sale Deed, Gift Deed, Will (Inheritance), or Exchange Deed.<br>Family Division: When property is divided through a registered Partition Deed (Vibhaga Pathra).<br>Surrendering Rights: When an owner surrenders claims through a registered Relinquishment Deed (Hakkubidugade Pathra) or Release Deed.<br>Legal / Court Mandates: When ownership is established or changed based on a Court Order or Decree.<br>Physical Changes to Property: When a building is newly constructed on a previously vacant site, or when there is an upgrade in the building type (e.g., from mud / sheet roof to RCC roof).<br>Amalgamation or Bifurcation: When combining multiple properties into one or dividing a single property into multiple units.<br>Change in Land Use: When a property transitions from residential to commercial use, or when agricultural land is legally transitioned via a Conversion Order.<br>Statutory Grants: When the government grants ownership rights under Sections 94C, 94CC, or 94D of the Land Revenue Act 1964 or Section 38A of the Karnataka Land Reforms Act 1961. Apply for e-Khata for New Layouts       <br>For a property within a new layout, apply for your e-Khata once your site has been released for registration- in either the 40% or 60% phase , and you hold your Conversion Order and Approved Layout Plan. <br>Registration requires technical approvals and infrastructure to be in place under the Karnataka Town and Country Planning (KTCP) regulations, and follows a phased timeline tied to the release of sites:<br>Phase 1 — Initial Registration (40% of Sites)<br>An e-Khata application may be initiated for the first 40% of sites once the developer obtains Preliminary Technical Layout Approval from the competent planning authority. The layout plan — including roads, parks, and civic amenity areas — must have been verified, and the transfer of public areas completed through a registered Relinquishment Deed.<br>Phase 2 — Final Registration (Remaining 60% of Sites)<br>The remaining 60% of sites are eligible only after all basic infrastructure works (roads, water supply, electricity) are completed. The developer must submit a Completion Certificate and a Defect Liability Period declaration. Upon issuance of Final Technical Layout Approval (Form XII), the Gram Panchayat must record the remaining sites within one month.<br>Registration in Form 11A<br>The applicant must hold a valid Land Conversion Order (Revenue Department) and Technical Layout Approval (Planning Authority). Upon receipt, the Gram Panchayat Secretary conducts field inspection and document verification, with the record finalised within 15 working days. Following PDO approval, a Property Identification Number (PID) is generated and the e-Khata is finalised in Form 11A. Mutation and Transfer (apply here for mutation for a property with existing e-khata)<br>In the case of the property already having an e-khata, Mutation and Transfer refer to the formal process of updating property records (Forms 9, 11A, or 11B) in the Gram Panchayat register when changes occur in property ownership, nature, or status.<br>CHANGE IN PROPERTY OWNERSHIP(for a property with existing e-khata)<br>Mutation is required when property rights are transferred through registered legal instruments, including:<br>• Sale Deed (Kraya Pathra): When a property is bought or sold.<br>• Gift Deed (Daana Pathra): When a property is gifted.<br>• Will / Inheritance (Uyilu Pathra / Pouthi Khaate): When property is transferred via a will or legal heirship.<br>• Relinquishment Deed (Hakku Bidugade Pathra / Tyaaga Pathra): When an owner surrenders rights to another party.<br>• Exchange Deed (Adalu Badalu Pathra): When properties are swapped between owners.<br>• Court Order / Decree: When ownership is established or changed by judicial mandate.<br>• Transfer (Vargaavane): General transfer of rights as recorded in official property documents.<br>PHYSICAL CHANGES TO THE PROPERTY( for a property with existing e-khata)<br>• Site to Building: When a building is newly constructed on a vacant plot.<br>• Building to Site: When a building is demolished, returning the property to a vacant plot status.<br>• Structural Upgrades: When the roof type changes (e.g., from mud or sheet to RCC roof).<br>CHANGES IN LAND USE OR CLASSIFICATION( for a property with existing e-khata)<br>• Usage Change: When a property transitions from Residential to Commercial use.<br>• Statutory Grants: When the government grants titles under the Land Revenue Act (Sections 94C, 94CC, 94D) or the Land Reforms Act (Section 38A).<br>ADMINISTRATIVE / LEGAL REORGANISATION( for a property with existing e-khata)<br>• Amalgamation: When two or more properties are combined into a single unit.<br>• Bifurcation / Partition: When a single property is divided among family members or heirs.<br>• Lease or Mortgage: When a property is leased, sub-leased, or mortgaged.<br>SPECIAL CATEGORIES – APARTMENTS / FLATS( for a property with existing e-khata)<br>• First Sale: The initial transfer from a Promoter / Developer to a Purchaser.<br>• Subsequent Sales: Any resale of a flat or apartment unit.<br>• Institutional Allotment: Sales or allotments by government bodies such as BDA or KHB.<br>For registered ownership changes (Sales, Gifts, etc.), the mutation and owner details must be updated in the digital system within 15 days of the application being processed.
